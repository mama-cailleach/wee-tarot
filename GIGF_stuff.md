# Wee Tarot - Controls and Descriptions

---

## Game controls

### Option 1 - Shortn'Sweet

**Wee Tarot - Controls**

**A** - confirm, advance, select  
**B** - back
**D-pad** - cycle through options / left side of diary  
**Crank** - shuffle deck / right side of diary  

Once done, please press the **Playdate menu button** on the upper right corner of the device and **Reset to Title**. 
Leave the table tidy for the next soul.

---

### Option 2 -  In Depth

**Wee Tarot - Controls**

**Starting out**  
**A** - start from title screen; advance through Dinah’s welcome  
**Up** - open Menu (How To, Sounds, Credits)  
**A** - begin a reading (Spread selection)  
**B** - open your Diary  

**Choosing a reading**  
**Up / Down** - move between Spread, Deck, and Seek  
**Left / Right** - change spread or deck filter  
**A** - cycle the highlighted row, or press **Seek** to begin  
**B** - back to the main screen  

Spreads: *1-bit Fortune · Root-Trunk-Branch · Pentagram · Celtic Cross · Horoscope*  
Decks: *Full · Major · Minor · individual suits · Alternate*

**During a reading**  
**Crank** - shuffle the deck (keep turning until it feels right)  
**A** - finish shuffling when prompted, then confirm your chosen card  
**Left / Right** - cycle through cards on the cloth  
**Up / Down** - zoom in and out on a card  

**The reading**  
**A** - advance through the fortune text  
**B** - on the last dialogue, look back at the cloth one last time

**Diary**  
**A** - unlock and open the diary  
**D-pad** - browse entries and zoom in and out cards (left side of the page)  
**Crank** - scroll text on the right-hand page  
**B** - go back / close  

**Everywhere else**  
**D-pad Up / Down** - move through menu lists  
**A** - confirm  
**B** - back  

Once done, please press the **Playdate menu button** on the upper right corner of the device and **Reset to Title**. 
Leave the table tidy for the next soul.

### Option 3 - Diegetic controls

**Wee Tarot - A few practical bits and bobs before fate gets dramatic**

If you’re not prompted to press a button, don’t fret. Trust the grid, it’s your guide.

The following overall rules hold for the lost:

Use the **d-pad** to cycle through choices.  
Press **A** to confirm and advance.  
Press **B** to back out when you need to.

In readings, **crank** to shuffle and wake the deck. When the rhythm feels true, press **A** to step forward.

When looking at the cloth, **Left** and **Right** cycle through the cards.  
**Up** and **Down** zoom in and out. Look closer before you ask them to speak.

Similarly for the Diary: the **d-pad** works the left side of the page; the **crank** turns the right.

Take your time. Rushing blurs the mind.  
And if the answer feels strange, ask again tomorrow.

When you’re finished, press the **Playdate menu button** on the upper right corner of the device and **Reset to Title**. 
Leave the table tidy for the next soul.

Thank you, darling...

### Option 2 - Bigger description

**Wee Tarot**  
*A Tarot Pull for the Curious Souls, the Skeptical Minds, and the Mildly Cursed*

Welcome, darling. The spirits have been expecting you, or maybe it’s just coincidence?

Shuffle the deck, stir the crank, and seek guidance for what troubles you. 

A tarot game for the Playdate with dry wit, ancient wisdom, and suspicious readings.

**This festival build is a preview** - the first outing of a major update before it goes live. What you’re playing today goes beyond the original release:

- **Five spreads** - from the original single *1-bit Fortune* to the new *Root-Trunk-Branch*, *Pentagram*, *Celtic Cross*, and *Horoscope*
- **Deck filters** - full deck, Major Arcana, Minor Arcana, individual suits, or an alternate deck
- **Zoom in option** - the same cards, just a wee bit bigger.
- **A personal diary** - past readings kept on the page (spread, date, cards, and whispers)
- **Updated** - menus, in-game how to guides, animations, art, sounds.

One draw or many. A glimpse or a whole layout. Nothing more... or is it?

*Not meant for serious life choices. If your card tells you to quit your job or fight a crow, please think twice. Especially about the crow.*

Made with crank-powered intention and love.
